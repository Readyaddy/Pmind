# User Interview Types for Product Managers

A reference guide for simulating and conducting the full spectrum of PM user interviews.
Each type includes purpose, structure, sample questions, and what to listen for.

---

## 1. Discovery / Problem Exploration Interview

**Goal:** Understand the user's world, frustrations, and unmet needs before any solution exists.

**When to use:** Early-stage research, exploring a new problem space, validating a problem hypothesis.

**Structure (45–60 min):**
1. Warm-up — understand their role, context (5 min)
2. Walk me through your day/workflow (10 min)
3. Dig into pain points: "tell me about the last time X was frustrating" (20 min)
4. Explore workarounds (10 min)
5. Wrap-up + anything you'd want us to know (5 min)

**Sample questions:**
- "Walk me through how you currently handle [problem area]."
- "What's the most time-consuming part of your week?"
- "Tell me about the last time [X] went wrong."
- "What workarounds have you built to deal with that?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing, what would it be?"

**What to listen for:** Frequency of pain, emotional language ("I hate", "drives me crazy"), workarounds (signals strong pain), and jobs-to-be-done.

---

## 2. Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD) Interview

**Goal:** Understand the "job" the user is trying to accomplish and what causes them to "hire" a product.

**When to use:** Positioning, feature prioritization, understanding switching behavior.

**Structure (45 min):**
1. Find the key purchase/adoption moment — "when did you first decide to use X?" (15 min)
2. Explore the before state — what triggered it, what were you using before (10 min)
3. Understand the anxieties and desired outcomes (10 min)
4. Explore the after state — what changed, what's still unsatisfying (10 min)

**Sample questions:**
- "Take me back to the moment you decided to start using [product]. What was happening?"
- "What were you using before? Why wasn't that working?"
- "What were you worried about when switching?"
- "How has your day-to-day changed since you started using it?"
- "What would make you stop using it?"

**What to listen for:** Push forces (why they left the old way), pull forces (what attracted them), anxieties, and habits holding them back.

---

## 3. Usability / Think-Aloud Interview

**Goal:** Observe how users interact with your product (or prototype), find friction, and validate UX assumptions.

**When to use:** Before launching a feature, after a redesign, validating a prototype.

**Structure (45–60 min):**
1. Brief context-setting (5 min)
2. Give a task scenario — don't say "click on X", give them a goal (30–40 min)
3. Debrief — what was hard, what was surprising (10 min)

**Sample questions:**
- "I'm going to give you a task. Please think out loud as you go — tell me what you're thinking."
- "What do you expect to happen when you click that?"
- "What would you normally do next?"
- "What's going through your mind right now?"
- "What would make this easier?"

**What to listen for:** Hesitation, confusion, unexpected paths, emotional reactions, moments of delight or frustration.

---

## 4. Concept Validation / Solution Interview

**Goal:** Test whether your proposed solution resonates before building it.

**When to use:** After discovery, when you have a solution hypothesis or mockup.

**Structure (45 min):**
1. Context-setting about their current state (10 min)
2. Show the concept / mockup (5 min)
3. Open-ended reaction first — "what do you see here?" (10 min)
4. Directed questions about value, comprehension, trust (15 min)
5. Wrap-up (5 min)

**Sample questions:**
- "Before I explain anything — what do you see here?"
- "Who do you think this is for?"
- "What would you use this for?"
- "What's missing? What's confusing?"
- "Would you pay for this? How much?"
- "What would need to be true for you to use this every day?"

**What to listen for:** Spontaneous understanding (no explanation needed), mismatch between your intent and their perception, "I already do this with X" signals.

## 5. Continuous Discovery Interview (Teresa Torres Style)

**Goal:** Maintain a constant pulse on customer needs by interviewing at least one user per week.

**When to use:** Ongoing — not tied to a specific project phase.

**Structure (20–30 min, lightweight):**
1. What are you working on right now? (5 min)
2. One pain point or struggle from the past week (10 min)
3. Specific story about that struggle (10 min)
4. (Optional) Share a concept for quick reaction (5 min)

**Sample questions:**
- "What's been the hardest thing you've dealt with this week?"
- "Tell me the story of the last time that happened."
- "What did you try first? What happened?"

**What to listen for:** Recurring themes across sessions, specific stories (not opinions), emerging needs before they become obvious.

## 6. Win/Loss Interview

**Goal:** Understand why customers chose or rejected your product — from people who just made a buying decision.

**When to use:** After sales close or churn events.

**Structure (30 min):**
1. Tell me about your evaluation process (10 min)
2. Who else did you evaluate? (5 min)
3. What were the deciding factors? (10 min)
4. Regrets or reservations? (5 min)

**Sample questions (Win):**
- "At what point did you feel confident about choosing us?"
- "What almost made you go with someone else?"
- "What did we do better than alternatives?"

**Sample questions (Loss):**
- "What didn't work for you about our product?"
- "What would we have needed to do to win your business?"
- "What did the product you chose do better?"

**What to listen for:** Specific feature gaps, pricing perception, sales process friction, positioning mismatch.

---

## 7. Churn / Cancellation Interview

**Goal:** Understand why users left so you can fix retention issues.

**When to use:** Within 2 weeks of a cancellation.

**Structure (20–30 min):**
1. What was your main use case when you signed up? (5 min)
2. Was the product meeting your needs? (10 min)
3. What triggered the decision to cancel? (10 min)
4. What would bring you back? (5 min)

**Sample questions:**
- "When did the product stop working for you?"
- "Was there a specific moment where you decided to cancel?"
- "What were you expecting that you didn't get?"
- "Did you find an alternative? What does it do differently?"

**What to listen for:** The "last straw" moment, unmet expectations from onboarding, product-market fit signals.

---

## 8. Onboarding / New User Interview

**Goal:** Understand first-time experience — what's confusing, what's missing, what creates the "aha" moment.

**When to use:** With users who signed up in the past 2–4 weeks.

**Structure (30–40 min):**
1. How did you hear about us? What made you sign up? (5 min)
2. Walk me through your first session (10 min)
3. What did you understand right away vs. what took effort? (10 min)
4. Did you reach a point where it "clicked"? (10 min)
5. What's still unclear or missing? (5 min)

**Sample questions:**
- "What were you hoping to accomplish when you first logged in?"
- "What was the first thing you tried to do?"
- "Was there a moment where it clicked and you understood the value?"
- "What almost made you quit before getting started?"

**What to listen for:** Time-to-value, confusing UX, false expectations, where users drop off.

---

## 9. Expert / Domain Interview

**Goal:** Build deep domain knowledge from subject matter experts (not necessarily your users).

**When to use:** Entering a new vertical, building B2B features for a specialized domain.

**Structure (45–60 min):**
1. Their background and how they got into the field (5 min)
2. How the industry / workflow currently works (20 min)
3. Pain points and inefficiencies (15 min)
4. How they see things changing (10 min)
5. Who else to talk to (5 min)

**Sample questions:**
- "How would you describe how [process] works to someone new to the industry?"
- "Where does the most time get wasted in this workflow?"
- "What do the best practitioners do differently from average ones?"
- "What do you wish existed that doesn't?"
- "Who in this field really gets it? Who should I talk to?"

**What to listen for:** Industry mental models, jargon to learn, systemic constraints, gaps in existing tooling.

---

## 10. Pricing Sensitivity Interview

**Goal:** Understand how users think about price and find the right price point.

**When to use:** Pre-launch pricing decisions, repositioning, testing new tiers.

**Structure (20–30 min):**
1. Current spend on alternatives / current budget (5 min)
2. Van Westendorp price sensitivity questions (15 min)
3. Value anchor — what outcome does it unlock? (10 min)

**Van Westendorp questions:**
- "At what price would this be so cheap that you'd question its quality?"
- "At what price would this be a bargain?"
- "At what price would this start to feel expensive, but you'd still consider it?"
- "At what price would this be too expensive to consider?"

**What to listen for:** Price anchors, what they're comparing to, the outcome they're really paying for.

---

## 11. Competitive Teardown Interview

**Goal:** Understand how users perceive your product vs. competitors from the user's mouth.

**When to use:** Competitive repositioning, understanding switching costs.

**Structure (30 min):**
1. Tools they currently use for this job (5 min)
2. Pros and cons of each tool they named (15 min)
3. Ideal product in their head (10 min)

**Sample questions:**
- "What tools are you using today for [job to be done]?"
- "If you had to rank them, what would be your #1? Why?"
- "What does [competitor] do better than anyone else?"
- "If you could take the best parts of each and combine them, what would that product look like?"

**What to listen for:** Category mental models, what "best in class" means to them, switching barriers.

---

## 12. Stakeholder / Internal User Interview

**Goal:** Understand the needs of internal users or stakeholders who influence product decisions.

**When to use:** Building internal tools, understanding adoption barriers, navigating organizational dynamics.

**Structure (30–45 min):**
1. Their role in relation to the product or workflow (10 min)
2. Current pain points or gaps in tools (15 min)
3. What success looks like for them (10 min)
4. What concerns or blockers do they have? (10 min)

**Sample questions:**
- "How do you use [product/process] today?"
- "What's your biggest challenge in getting your job done?"
- "What would success look like in 6 months?"
- "What would make you resistant to changing the current process?"
- "If you could change one thing, what would it be?"

**What to listen for:** Political concerns, hidden requirements, misalignment between what they say and what they do.

## Quick Reference: Interview Type Selection Matrix

| Situation | Best interview type |
|-----------|-------------------|
| "We don't know what to build" | Discovery, JTBD |
| "We have a mockup to test" | Concept validation, Usability |
| "Why are users churning?" | Churn interview |
| "New users aren't activating" | Onboarding interview |
| "We lost a deal" | Win/Loss |
| "What should we charge?" | Pricing sensitivity |
| "We're entering a new market" | Expert / domain |
| "How do we compare to competitors?" | Competitive teardown |
| "We need to keep a pulse on users" | Continuous discovery |
| "Internal tool adoption is low" | Stakeholder interview |
