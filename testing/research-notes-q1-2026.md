# Shopflow — Q1 2026 User Research Notes

**Product:** Shopflow (B2B inventory & order management for small e-commerce brands)
**Conducted by:** Maya R. (Product) — Jan 8–22, 2026
**Sample:** 5 interviews across customer segments (small brand → mid-market multi-channel)

## Recurring themes (rough count)

- Dashboard performance / filter persistence (3/5 interviews)
- Onboarding & SKU mapping pain (2/5)
- Pricing tier mismatch — especially mid-market gap (3/5)
- Mobile / warehouse-floor reliability (2/5)
- Integration breakage (Shopify OAuth, QuickBooks sync) (3/5)
- Reporting / export limitations (2/5)
- Notification noise → muted (1/5, but high severity — caused stockouts)
- Bulk operations missing (2/5)

## Hottest quotes for stakeholder share-out

- "It's the kind of thing that makes you hate software." — Daniela, on filters resetting
- "Net negative feature." — Petar, on Slack alert noise causing missed stockouts
- "Six thousand units re-counted." — Aanya, on warehouse scanner connection drops
- "I budgeted $400/mo and we're paying $680." — Marcus, on hidden seat fees
- "If a competitor launches a more honest pricing tier in the next 90 days, I'm gone." — Jen-Hsuan

## Cross-cutting observations

- 3 of 5 interviewees explicitly mentioned a competitor by name (Linnworks, Cin7, Stitch). Churn risk is concrete, not hypothetical.
- The forecasting model is universally praised when it works — there is product trust to draw on for the harder workflow fixes.
- Mid-market customers (Growth tier, $399) feel they're paying for features they can't use, while small customers (Starter, $99) hit tier walls almost immediately. The tier gap is the loudest pricing complaint.
- Mobile / warehouse-floor reliability is the highest-severity issue surfaced (caused inventory write-offs at Lumen Lighting).
