USER INTERVIEW — Petar L., Head of Ops, "Atlas Outdoor"
Company: Outdoor goods, ~40k orders/mo, multi-channel
Product: Shopflow
Interviewer: Maya R. (Product)
Date: Jan 22, 2026

═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Maya: You've been with us almost two years. What keeps you?

Petar: Switching cost, mostly. I'm being honest with you. We've trained 18
people on Shopflow. Migrating that knowledge to another platform is a
quarter of work I don't have.

Maya: What are your biggest current frictions?

Petar: The reports module. I cannot export a custom report to CSV. The PDF
export works, but my finance team needs it in Sheets for the consolidated
P&L. I literally screenshot the table and OCR it. That's medieval. There
must be a button I can ask for that takes one engineer a day.

Maya: What else?

Petar: The notification system. We get a Slack ping for every single low-
stock alert. No way to digest them, no way to group by supplier, no way
to mute below a threshold. So we muted the whole channel six months ago.
Which means we missed three actual stockouts last month. Net negative
feature.

Maya: That's a great phrase — "net negative feature".

Petar: Put it on a t-shirt. Look — I want to be clear, the core product is
good. Inventory tracking is accurate to a degree I never had with Stitch.
But the surrounding workflows — alerts, exports, mobile, bulk edit — feel
like they were built by someone who's never run a warehouse.

Maya: If I gave you one wish?

Petar: Saved filter views. With names. Shared across the team. That alone
saves us 30 hours a month.
