But here's the honest gap. Line your features up against the loop and you've built Ingest and most of the Evidence layer, plus the editor around it. The two boxes that are the actual moat are thin or missing:

Your evidence layer looks per-batch, not longitudinal. You harvest quotes and sort them into themes each time you ingest — but nothing on the page says the system recognizes the same pain point recurring across six months and trending up. That stateful recurrence is the moat; batch synthesis is commoditized (it's what AI PM OS does for $99).
There's no decision ledger — RICE scores an opportunity once, but nothing logs it as a tracked bet with a predicted outcome and a revisit date.
There's no outcome capture at all. Nothing pulls the actual metric after you ship and scores whether your bet was right. That's the box competitors can't copy, and it's the one you haven't built.

So, ranked by leverage:
Tier 1 — make the evidence layer stateful (highest leverage, smallest lift). You already extract and tag insights. The change is to persist and dedupe across time: when new feedback matches an existing theme, increment it rather than re-creating it, and surface recurrence + trend ("mentioned by 23 users, up 40% this quarter, you've shipped nothing against it"). This converts work you already do into the compounding asset, and it's your day-1 hook that also seeds the moat. Do this first.
Tier 2 — turn committed opportunities into a decision ledger. You have the opportunity/RICE object already; extend it. When a PM commits to building something, capture: rationale, the evidence it rests on (link back to the graph), a predicted impact on the north-star metric, and a revisit date. Now "why did we build this" is answerable forever — that's your switching cost.
Tier 3 — outcome capture + calibration (deepest moat, longest payoff). Integrate PostHog/Amplitude/Mixpanel. On the revisit date, pull the actual metric, compare to prediction, score it. Build the data model for this now even if the UI is minimal — you want to start accruing calibration data immediately, because it's worthless until it has months of history.
What to cut or defer — you're sprawling. Multimodal UX audits (screenshot review) and "Collaborative Strategy" are commoditized side-features that dilute a solo founder's focus and aren't on the critical path to the moat. The savings calculator and the "$57,824/year" framing compete on time saved — the exact axis AI PM OS and every wrapper also claim, so it's a weak differentiator. Park all three.
Positioning fix. Your FAQ literally opens with "Is this just another generic ChatGPT wrapper?" — you know that's the objection, and right now your defense (multi-agent backend, RICE hierarchies) is checks the product's moat can't yet cash. Stop defending on "we're not a wrapper" and stop leading with "time saved." Reposition around the thing nothing else does: the only PM workspace that remembers every decision and tells you whether it actually worked. Memory and accountability, not speed. That headline is one nobody on your competitive set can honestly claim.
